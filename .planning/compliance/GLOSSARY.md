## 1. Purpose

This glossary maps key terms from Israeli privacy and data protection legislation to their English equivalents as used throughout the Tenuto.io compliance document set. All compliance documents are written in English, but they reference Israeli regulations which are published in Hebrew. This glossary enables auditors, developers, and compliance reviewers to cross-reference between the English compliance documents and the original Hebrew regulatory texts.

The primary sources for these terms are:

- **Hok Haganat HaPratiyut, 5741-1981** (Privacy Protection Law)
- **Takkanot Haganat HaPratiyut (Abtakhat Meida BeM'aarechot Meida), 5777-2017** (Privacy Protection Regulations -- Information Security in Databases)
- **Takkanot Haganat HaPratiyut (Haavalat Meida LeMa'arakhot Meida SheMehuts LeGvulot HaMedina), 5761-2001** (Cross-Border Data Transfer Regulations)
- **Hok HaGanat HaPratiyut (Tikun Mispar 13), 5783-2023** (Privacy Protection Amendment No. 13)

## 2. Privacy Law and Regulatory Terms

| Hebrew Term | Transliteration | English Term | Definition / Usage Context |
|-------------|----------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| הגנת הפרטיות | Haganat HaPratiyut | Privacy Protection | The overarching legal framework for personal data protection in Israel |
| חוק הגנת הפרטיות | Hok Haganat HaPratiyut | Privacy Protection Law | The primary statute (1981, as amended) governing collection, use, and protection of personal data |
| תקנות הגנת הפרטיות (אבטחת מידע) | Takkanot Haganat HaPratiyut (Abtakhat Meida) | Privacy Protection Regulations (Information Security) | The 2017 regulations specifying security requirements for databases containing personal data |
| מאגר מידע | Ma'agar Meida | Database / Information Repository | A collection of personal data as defined under the Privacy Protection Law; requires registration above a threshold |
| רישום מאגר מידע | Rishum Ma'agar Meida | Database Registration | The requirement to register certain databases with the Privacy Protection Authority |
| מידע אישי | Meida Ishi | Personal Data / Personal Information | Any information relating to an identified or identifiable individual |
| מידע רגיש | Meida Ragish | Sensitive Data | Data categories receiving enhanced protection: health, genetics, sexual orientation, political opinions, religious beliefs, criminal record |
| נושא המידע | Noseh HaMeida | Data Subject | The individual to whom the personal data relates |
| בעל מאגר המידע | Ba'al Ma'agar HaMeida | Database Owner / Data Controller | The entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data |
| מחזיק מאגר המידע | Mahzik Ma'agar HaMeida | Database Holder / Data Processor | The entity that processes personal data on behalf of the database owner |
| הסכם עיבוד מידע | Heskem Ibud Meida | Data Processing Agreement (DPA) | A contractual agreement between controller and processor defining data handling obligations |
| הסכמה | Haskama | Consent | Voluntary, informed agreement by the data subject to the processing of their personal data |
| הסכמה מדעת | Haskama MiDa'at | Informed Consent | Consent given with full knowledge of the purposes and implications of data processing |
| מטרת העיבוד | Matarat HaIbud | Purpose of Processing | The specific, legitimate reason for collecting and processing personal data |
| העברת מידע לחו"ל | Ha'avalat Meida LeKhul | Cross-Border Data Transfer | Transfer of personal data to a jurisdiction outside Israel |

## 7. Usage Notes

1. **Translation conventions:** Where Israeli legal terms have no direct English equivalent, the closest functional equivalent is used with the Hebrew term noted. For example, "Ma'agar Meida" is rendered as "Database" in compliance documents but refers specifically to the legal concept of a registered information repository under Israeli law, which is broader than a technical database.

2. **Gendered terms:** Hebrew uses grammatical gender for roles. Both forms are listed where applicable (e.g., Talmid/Talmida). In compliance documents, English gender-neutral terms are used.

3. **Regulatory citations:** When compliance documents reference specific regulations, the Hebrew title is provided alongside the English translation at first mention, then the English term is used throughout. This glossary provides the mapping for all such references.

4. **Abbreviations:** The abbreviation sha"sh (Weekly Hours) appears frequently in the codebase and represents a fundamental unit of measure in Israeli music education administration. The hours_summary collection is named after this concept.

5. **Cross-referencing:** To find the Hebrew source text for any English term used in the compliance documents, locate the term in the "English Term" column of the appropriate table above, then use the Hebrew term to search the original legislation at the Israeli Knesset legislation database (https://knesset.gov.il) or the Privacy Protection Authority website (https://www.gov.il/he/departments/the_privacy_protection_authority).
